Acknowledgement of Country

Understanding and practising respectful acknowledgement of the Traditional Custodians of the land where events take place.

What It Means

More Than a Formality

An Acknowledgement of Country is a formal statement recognising the Traditional Custodians of the land where a gathering takes place. It acknowledges their enduring connection to Country — the land, waters, sky, and community.

Anyone can deliver an Acknowledgement of Country. You do not need to be an Aboriginal or Torres Strait Islander person to do so, but you should deliver it with genuine understanding, not as a rote phrase.

Key Distinction

Acknowledgement vs Welcome to Country

Acknowledgement of Country
  • Can be delivered by anyone
  • A sign of respect and recognition
  • Common at events, meetings, and gatherings
Welcome to Country
  • Can ONLY be performed by a Traditional Custodian or Elder
  • A formal ceremony welcoming visitors to their Country
  • Requires coordination with local Elders

Common Mistakes to Avoid

Being tokenistic, rushing through it without meaning. Using the wrong Country name for the location. Treating it as a formality by reading words off a page without thought. Using the same generic statement everywhere regardless of location.

Finding Custodians Outside Capital Cities

Check your local council website, contact your local Land Council, or use the AIATSIS Map of Indigenous Australia to find the Traditional Custodians of any location.
